Documentation & Purpose
CADS (Context-Aware Dynamic Slack) is an algorithm designed for Mobile and Embedded Systems. It utilizes DVFS (Dynamic Voltage and Frequency Scaling) to exploit 'Slack Time'—the gap between a task's completion and its deadline. By running at the lowest viable frequency, we achieve cubic reductions in power consumption ($P \propto V^2f$).
Project Features
• V5.0 Power Budgeting: Monitors global power draw to ensure the system stays within hardware thermal limits.
• Leakage Mitigation: Simulates the trade-off between scaling frequency and 'Race to Sleep' strategies.
• Preemptive Scaling: Adjusts frequencies in real-time as high-priority tasks enter the queue.
